Info

Christmas is celebrated on December 25 and is both a sacred religious holiday and a worldwide cultural and commercial phenomenon. For two millennia, people around the world have been observing it with traditions and practices that are both religious and secular in nature. Christians celebrate Christmas Day as the anniversary of the birth of Jesus of Nazareth, a spiritual leader whose teachings form the basis of their religion. Popular customs include exchanging gifts, decorating Christmas trees, attending church, sharing meals with family and friends and, of course, waiting for Santa Claus to arrive. December 25—Christmas Day—has been a federal holiday in the United States since 1870.

Christmas
Date: Christmas is celebrated on December 25th each year. Religious Significance: For Christians, Christmas is a religious holiday marking the birth of Jesus Christ in Bethlehem. The word "Christmas" comes from the Old English phrase "Cristes Maesse," which means "Christ's Mass." Traditions and Customs: Decorations: Many people decorate their homes with Christmas trees, lights, and ornaments. Mistletoe and holly are also popular decorations. Gift-Giving: Exchanging gifts is a common Christmas tradition, symbolizing the gifts the Magi brought to the baby Jesus. Feasting: Christmas is often associated with special meals and feasts. Traditional Christmas foods vary by region but may include roast turkey, ham, stuffing, and festive desserts. Christmas Carols: Singing Christmas carols is a popular tradition. Many songs tell the story of the birth of Jesus or convey the festive spirit of the season. Santa Claus: The figure of Santa Claus, also known as Saint Nicholas or Kris Kringle, is a central part of Christmas in many cultures. He is believed to bring gifts to children on Christmas Eve. Christmas Eve and Christmas Day: Christmas Eve, on December 24th, is often celebrated with family gatherings, festive meals, and midnight church services. Christmas Day is a public holiday in many countries, and celebrations continue with more family time, gift exchanges, and special meals. Secular Celebrations: While Christmas has religious roots, it is also celebrated by people of various faiths and cultures in a more secular manner. Many people around the world, regardless of religious affiliation, participate in the festive spirit of the season. Global Celebrations: Christmas is celebrated globally, but traditions and customs can vary widely from country to country. Each culture adds its unique touch to the holiday festivities. Symbols: Common symbols associated with Christmas include the Christmas tree, ornaments, angels, stars, and nativity scenes depicting the birth of Jesus.
